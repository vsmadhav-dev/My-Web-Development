Subhash Chandra Bose

A man no one talks about

Here is a brief History of Subhash Chandra Bose

Early Life and Education (1897–1921)

  • Birth: Born on January 23, 1897, in Cuttack, Odisha, to Janakinath Bose and Prabhavati Dutt.
  • Academic Excellence: A brilliant student, he studied at Presidency College, Calcutta. He later went to England and ranked 4th in the prestigious Indian Civil Service (ICS) exam in 1920.
  • The Turning Point: Deeply moved by the nationalist movement and the Jallianwala Bagh massacre, he resigned from the ICS in 1921, stating, "No real change in history has ever been achieved by discussions."

Entry into Politics (1921–1937)

  • Mentorship: Upon returning to India, he joined the Indian National Congress (INC) under the mentorship of Chittaranjan Das.
  • Escape from India: Under house arrest in 1941, Bose escaped in disguise (the "Great Escape"), traveling through Afghanistan and the USSR to reach Germany.
  • The INA: In 1943, he reached Singapore and took command of the Indian National Army (Azad Hind Fauj), originally founded by Rash Behari Bose and Captain Mohan Singh.
  • Provisional Government: He formed the Arzi Hukumat-e-Azad Hind (Provisional Government of Free India) in Singapore.
  • Battle Cry: It was during this period he gave the famous slogans "Jai Hind" and "Give me blood, and I shall give you freedom!"

The Final Mystery and Independence (1945–1947)

  • The Disappearance: Following Japan's surrender in WWII, Bose reportedly died in a plane crash in Taihoku (Taiwan) on August 18, 1945. However, many Indians refused to believe this, leading to decades of conspiracy theories.
  • Legacy: Though he did not live to see the tricolor hoisted in 1947, the INA Trials at the Red Fort in 1945-46 triggered massive unrest within the British Indian Army and Navy, significantly accelerating the British decision to leave India.
